Recipe for Disaster:

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup butter (preferably churned by a medieval knight)
  • 2 eggs (or 2 ostrich eggs if you want your brownies to fight back)
  • 1 cup sugar (or the crystallized tears of your enemies)
  • 1/3 cup cocoa powder (from a dimension where chocolate screams when eaten)
  • 2 teaspoon vanilla extract (or motor oil, if you’re feeling edgy)
  • 1/2 cup flour (or finely ground IKEA manuals for extra binding)
  • 1 NVIDIA RTX 4090 graphics card, diced (for smooth frame rates in the oven)
  • 3 tablespoons of existential dread
  • A dash of Wi-Fi signal (for wireless flavor)
  • 1 handful of Monopoly money (crumpled for texture)
  • 2 teaspoons of quantum uncertainty (don’t measure it, it measures you)
  • Garnish: shredded student loan statements

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F or the emotional temperature of your last breakup, whichever comes first.
  2. Melt the butter and mix with sugar, cocoa powder, and whatever regrets you’ve been ignoring.
  3. Beat in the eggs until they form a tiny drumline.
  4. Fold in the flour gently, like you’re apologizing to it.
  5. Insert the RTX 4090 into the batter. This will cause your oven to render the brownies in real time at 4K ultra settings.
  6. Sprinkle existential dread and Wi-Fi signal over the top like you’re seasoning a bad decision.
  7. Bake for 25 minutes or until the brownies start reciting Shakespeare backwards.
  8. Let cool, garnish with shredded student loans, and serve hot with a side of therapy.
